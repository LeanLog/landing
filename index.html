02 — How operations run today

Your systems hold the records. Your team holds the work.

In every operations-heavy business, the actual logic of the business lives outside the system of record. Migrating won't fix it.

01 / 03

Your ERP knows your customers. It doesn't run the work for them.

Customers, suppliers, products, prices — all in the system. But the actual work — quoting an order, scheduling production, chasing a supplier, reconciling an invoice — happens outside it, in your team's email, spreadsheets, and phone calls.

02 / 03

The work lives between the systems.

A PO arrives by email. Your team keys it in, calls the supplier, updates the spreadsheet, follows up. The PO ends up in the system. The decisions — which supplier, why this exception, when to escalate — stay outside it.

03 / 03

Knowledge walks out the door.

When somebody leaves, part of the operation goes with them. The spreadsheets stay. The reasons behind them, why this supplier, why this lane, why this exception, do not.

03 — How it runs

Runs your back office the way a great ops hire would.

No new software to learn. No process changes. The agent works through your existing channels — phone, email, ERP, spreadsheets — and slots in alongside your team.

01 — talk

You make the decisions. The agent makes the moves.

Send the agent a task — "quote Apex," "reconcile Cosmo's invoice," "reorder BX-208" — and it sources the data, surfaces the trade-offs, and presents the call you need to make. You decide; the agent then handles the calls, emails, paperwork, and follow-ups. Every task is a short conversation, not a workflow handoff.

02 — capture

Every supplier choice, every exception, every decision rule the agent learns becomes part of your operation's codified logic. The next hire inherits both the data and the operating instructions. Turnover stops being a knowledge crisis.
